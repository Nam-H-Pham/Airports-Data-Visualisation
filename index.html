Airports per Million People

It is important to note that the number of airports in a country is not necessarily a reflection of the country's size or population. For example, the United States has the most airports in the world, but it is also the third largest country by area and the fourth most populous country. Brazil, on the other hand, is the fifth largest country by area and the sixth most populous country, but it has the second most airports in the world.

Airports of the World: A Location Map

The number of airports in a country is also influenced by a number of other factors, such as the country's geography, economy, and transportation infrastructure. For example, countries with mountainous terrain or remote areas are more likely to have more airports than countries with flat terrain and well-developed transportation infrastructure.

Comparing Airport Types by Continent



Small airports and heliports are the most abundant because they are less expensive to build and operate, more flexible in terms of location, more convenient for local communities, and more versatile than large airports. They play an important role in the global transportation system by providing access to air travel for people and businesses in rural and remote areas.

Flight Routes of the World

Airports decide on flight routes based on a variety of factors, including distance, weather, wind, air traffic control, overflight fees, passenger demand, noise pollution, and environmental impact. For example, Sydney Airport has developed flight routes that minimize noise pollution, while London Heathrow Airport has to carefully manage the flow of traffic due to its high volume of flights. Dubai International Airport, on the other hand, is a major hub for long-haul flights, so airlines flying to and from Dubai often choose routes that take advantage of tailwinds to reduce fuel consumption and flight time.
